Defense in layers

The HitaVir Retail lake sits at the center in its medallion hues. Five protective layers wrap around it, each rose-toned ring doing one job. Together they decide who gets in, what they can see, whether the bytes are scrambled, and who is watching.

The data lake Protective layers Foundation storage

Rings around the lake

Each layer must be passed before the data is reached. Hover a ring to lift it.

IAM

Decides who can act and grants each identity only the permissions it needs — least privilege.

Lake Formation

Governs the catalog down to the column, so an analyst reads customers but never the email field.

KMS

Encrypts the data at rest with a key you own, so the bytes on disk are unreadable without it.

CloudWatch

Collects metrics and logs and fires alarms — tells you the moment a Glue job fails or costs spike.

CloudTrail

Records every API call into an audit log, so you always know who did what, when, and from where.

The lake (center)

Amazon S3 holds the medallion layers. Everything above exists to protect and watch it.
